Running Red Lights: Seat Belt On

I self-published an autobiography of sorts. A short book on coming-of-age, blah, blah. It sounds much more terrible than it actually is, but I will still try my best to make it sound appealing:

Second edition. "Make new friends, make new friends. Melody is nice, but you should make new friends. I didn't want to, but I agreed. What else was suppose to say?" Old English freondscipe "friendship, mutual liking and regard," also "conjugal love;". Conjugal meant of or relating to marriage or the relationship of a married couple. Old English freond, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch vriend and German Freund, from an Indo-European root meaning 'to love,'. Surely such words were meant for a deep relationship . . . Growing up is hard. Briana Washington is an easily-forgettable black girl that has a crippling disability of making friends. But she also has to learn about being a woman, and just what the heck are boys anyway? There's coping with anxiety attacks and random flairs of depression, and lots of instances of being in the wrong place at the wrong time.
